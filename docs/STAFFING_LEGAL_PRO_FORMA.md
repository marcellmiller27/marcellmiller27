# John Henry Investments Staffing and Legal Pro Forma

## Purpose

This document estimates realistic staffing needs, founder/operator feasibility, legal expenditures, compliance expenditures, and staffing-adjusted pro forma impact for the John Henry Investments platform.

These are planning estimates, not legal, tax, accounting, compensation, or investment advice. Actual expenses depend on jurisdiction, regulatory status, hiring strategy, compensation, legal counsel, customer mix, automation, integrations, and whether the platform provides educational tools or regulated advisory services.

Detailed quarterly and annual compensation and professional-services projections are documented in `docs/COMPENSATION_AND_PRO_SERVICES_PROJECTIONS.md`.

Department-level job descriptions, skill requirements, education/experience expectations, and legal personnel requirements are documented in `docs/JOB_DESCRIPTIONS_AND_STAFFING_REQUIREMENTS.md`.

## Can this be operated by one person?

Short answer:

```text
Yes for prototype and limited controlled beta.
No for a production financial SaaS platform with paying users, integrations, uploaded financial documents, AI analysis, billing, and compliance obligations.
```

## One-person operating feasibility

| Stage | Can one person operate it? | Practical notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Prototype | Yes | One person can manage product, demos, docs, and limited testing. |
| Controlled beta | Possibly | One person can operate if users are few, support is light, uploads are limited, and legal/compliance risk is controlled. |
| Paid MVP | Not recommended | One person becomes a bottleneck across support, security, billing, reporting, integrations, legal coordination, and product development. |
| Growth | No | Requires dedicated engineering, support, compliance, product, and operations coverage. |
| Enterprise scale | No | Requires formal departments, security program, compliance program, support operations, and account management. |

## Staffing/legal-adjusted EBITDA outlook

The previous EBITDA model estimated EBITDA before staffing, customer acquisition, and many professional services. The table below adjusts the base blended SaaS scenario by adding realistic staffing and legal/compliance costs.

Base blended SaaS mix:

```text
85% Consumer, 13% Professional, 2% Enterprise
```

| Tier | Monthly revenue | Platform operating costs incl. payment processing | Staffing/professional services | Legal/compliance recurring | Adjusted monthly EBITDA | Adjusted annual EBITDA |
| --- | ---: | ---: | ---: | ---: | ---: | ---: |
| Prototype - 100 users | $11K | $434-$1,084 | $0-$15K | $0-$2.5K | ($6.5K)-$10.7K | ($78K)-$128K |
| Paid MVP - 1,000 users | $111K | $5.8K-$15.3K | $35K-$90K | $2K-$10K | ($4K)-$68K | ($48K)-$816K |
| Growth - 10,000 users | $1.114M | $58K-$158K | $250K-$650K | $25K-$100K | $206K-$781K | $2.47M-$9.37M |
| Scale - 50,000 users | $5.569M | $317K-$817K | $1.0M-$2.5M | $100K-$300K | $1.95M-$4.15M | $23.4M-$49.8M |
| Enterprise - 100,000 users | $11.137M | $734K-$1.834M | $2.25M-$6.0M | $250K-$800K | $2.50M-$7.90M | $30.0M-$94.8M |

Interpretation:

- One person can preserve cash in prototype stage, but capacity and compliance risk are limited.
- Paid MVP can be profitable if staffing remains lean and professional/enterprise revenue appears early.
- Paid MVP can also be negative if legal, support, and engineering costs scale before revenue.
- Growth and scale tiers can support meaningful EBITDA if acquisition costs, AI usage, and support costs are controlled.

## Practical recommendation

For John Henry Investments, the most realistic near-term operating plan is:

```text
Founder-led controlled beta + outside counsel + contractor engineering + limited AI/document usage.
```

Recommended initial monthly budget excluding founder salary:

```text
$5,000-$20,000/month
```

Recommended initial legal/security budget:

```text
$25,000-$100,000 before broad paid launch
```

Recommended paid MVP staffing budget:

```text
$35,000-$90,000/month
```

The platform should not broadly launch paid financial workflows until legal disclaimers, privacy terms, data handling, security controls, and user consent for integrations are reviewed.
