# Build It Yourself (Founder‑Led, Lean) vs Outsource — Realistic Assessment

Question: is it better for us to **build the SaaS platform ourselves** (founder‑led,
lean) to obtain users — rather than outsourcing? Honest answer and operating plan.

## Short answer

**Yes — build and own the core yourself (founder + AI agents).** It is the leanest,
highest‑ownership path, it preserves equity/IP and cash, and a working platform
*already exists* from this project. **But reframe the real problem:** building is now
the cheap, controllable part; **obtaining users (distribution) is the hard part** —
and that is where most solo SaaS founders fail, not the build.

## Why build‑it‑yourself wins for a lean founder

| Dimension | Build yourself (founder + AI) | Outsource the build |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Cash cost | ~$0 labor + ~$0.6–3.6k/mo infra/AI | $30k–250k+ project / squad |
| Equity & IP | 100% retained | retained if contracted well, but risk |
| Speed to change | minutes/hours (you + AI) | days + vendor coordination |
| Control & learning | total; you learn your own product | partial; knowledge leaves with vendor |
| Key‑person risk | **high (you are the bottleneck)** | lower for build, higher lock‑in |

With AI coding/support agents, a lean founder can build, ship, and maintain a real
product at near‑zero labor cost — exactly what produced this platform. Handing the
whole build to an agency would spend $30–250k to recreate something you can own.

## The honest, realistic outcome

1. **The build is no longer the bottleneck.** You can ship and iterate an MVP solo
   with AI. Expect to reach a deployable paid MVP in weeks, not quarters.
2. **Distribution is the bottleneck.** A great product with no users fails. Plan to
   spend the **majority of your founder time on getting users**, not on features.
3. **Finance has non‑negotiables.** You are handling money and personal/financial
   data. Before real customers: a **security review, secure auth (already built),
   data protection, and basic compliance posture** are mandatory. This is the one
   area to bring in **fractional senior expertise** — do not rely on AI alone for
   security/architecture/incident response.
4. **Key‑person risk is the quiet danger.** If you're the only one who understands
   the system, illness/burnout stalls everything. Mitigate with documentation
   (`docs/CODE_OBJECTIVES.md`), tests, and a fractional engineer on call.

Realistic success determinant: **founder distribution + domain credibility**, not who
writes the code.

## Recommended lean founder operating model

- **You (founder):** product, pricing, distribution, customer relationships,
  compliance ownership.
- **AI coding agent:** build/iterate the platform (as in this project).
- **AI support agent:** 24/7 tier‑1 customer service (already built: `/support`).
- **Fractional senior engineer / security advisor (a few hrs/mo):** architecture,
  security review, incident readiness — the finance non‑negotiable.
- **Performance‑based growth help (optional):** a fintech growth partner paid on
  results for user acquisition (see `docs/OUTSOURCING_PHASE1_MVP_PLAN.md`).

This keeps fixed cash at the lean ~$1.6–3.6k/mo from `docs/CASHFLOW_PROJECTION_12MO.md`
while putting expert help only where it's truly required.

## What to outsource (selectively) even if you build

- **Security review / penetration test** before taking real money.
- **Specialized integrations** (e.g., licensed market/fundamentals data, KYC/AML).
- **Growth/CRO** on performance terms — distribution is the thing worth paying for.

Do **not** outsource: product vision, pricing, data ownership, or customer
relationships.

## Realistic milestones (founder + AI, lean)

1. **Weeks 1–6:** finish/polish prototype, wire live data, public landing + waitlist,
   instrument analytics. *Goal: validate interest.*
2. **Weeks 6–14:** harden paid MVP (billing live, security review, AI support),
   launch first acquisition channel. *Goal: first paying users.*
3. **Months 4–12:** double down on the one channel that converts; reinvest the cash
   the lean model preserves into growth; hire only against the demand triggers.

## Failure modes to avoid

- **Building forever, never shipping** — set ship dates; distribution beats polish.
- **Great product, no distribution** — start acquisition *before* the build is "done."
- **Skipping security/compliance** — fatal in finance; get the fractional expert.
- **Founder burnout / single point of failure** — document, test, keep help on call.

## Recommendation

**Build and own it yourself, lean (founder + AI agents)** — it's cheaper, faster, and
keeps 100% equity/IP, and the platform already exists. **Spend your scarce founder
time and any budget on distribution**, bring in **fractional senior security/eng**
because it's finance, and use **performance‑based growth help** to obtain users. Only
outsource specialized gaps — never the vision or the customer.
