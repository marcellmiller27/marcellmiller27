# Mission vs. Ethos — Review, Comparison, and Recommendation

A review of the proposed statement, a comparison to the existing mission statement
(`docs/MISSION_STATEMENT.md`), and a clear recommendation on how to use each.

## The proposed statement (as submitted)

> Our focus is presenting the latest most innovative technology in today's
> ever‑changing world. We have a dedicated research department that scrutinizes all
> new projects entering the finance or crypto industry. We understand that everyday
> hard‑working individuals are looking for solid opportunities to invest their
> resources in. So take a moment and give us a chance to enlighten your horizons and
> together we will change the world. What is your purpose? Why do you do what you do
> daily? Do you understand what it means to be happy and have true joy, comfortable
> in your own skin? Are you maximizing your time in everything you do? Do you
> understand the value of time and not taking it for granted? How many times have you
> heard the phrase, "I just don't have time"? Did you know there are 168 hours in a
> week?

### Review

**Strengths**
- Warm, human, and motivational; speaks directly to "everyday hard‑working" people.
- Communicates two real differentiators: **innovation** and a **dedicated research /
  due‑diligence** function for finance *and* crypto.
- The **time / "168 hours"** idea is a memorable, ownable hook.

**Weaknesses (as a *mission statement*)**
- Too long and covers several distinct ideas (tech, research, empathy, purpose,
  happiness, time) — a mission statement should be one crisp sentence or two.
- Heavy use of second‑person rhetorical questions (purpose, joy, time) — that is
  **manifesto / brand‑voice** material, not a mission.
- Aspirations like "change the world" / "enlighten your horizons" are inspiring but
  vague; a mission needs a concrete *what* and *for whom*.
- Minor copy fixes: "today's", add question marks, tighten run‑ons.

## The existing mission statement (for comparison)

> **Creed:** Institutional intelligence. Family legacy.
> **Mission:** To compound and protect capital across generations. John Henry
> Investments unites institutional‑grade research, disciplined risk management, and
> our proprietary Opportunity Score across public markets, private businesses, real
> assets, and digital assets — turning conviction into durable, multi‑generational
> wealth for the families and allocators we serve.

This is concise, outcome‑focused (preserve + compound), names the *what* and the
*for whom*, and reads with fiduciary credibility — i.e., it already does the job a
mission statement should.

## Verdict

**Use the proposed statement as the company ETHOS / manifesto — not as the mission.**
The two pieces do different jobs and are stronger together:

- **Mission statement** = the formal, concise *what we do and for whom* (investor
  decks, About page header, legal/boilerplate). Keep it tight.
- **Ethos / manifesto** = the emotional, second‑person *why we exist and how we think*
  (landing hero, About story, brand voice). The proposed statement, polished, is an
  excellent ethos.

They should not be merged into one paragraph; merging dilutes both.

## A1 refinement — broaden beyond crypto (decision pending)

Feedback: replace "crypto" with "technology" to cover the whole industry. Note: a
literal "markets and technology" repeats "technology" (already in "financial
technology") and can read as the *tech sector* (narrower). Better: name the breadth
as **every asset class** and let **technology** describe the edge.

- **A1‑refined (recommended):** *To put institutional‑grade research and modern
  technology in the hands of everyday investors and the allocators who serve them —
  scrutinizing opportunities across every asset class, from public markets to private
  businesses, real assets, and digital assets, so people can invest their time and
  capital with confidence and build durable, multi‑generational wealth.*
- **A1‑concise:** *To put institutional‑grade research and modern technology in the
  hands of everyday investors — scrutinizing opportunities across every asset class so
  people can invest their time and capital with confidence and build durable,
  multi‑generational wealth.*
- **A1‑tech‑forward:** *To harness the latest technology and institutional‑grade
  research so everyday investors and allocators can evaluate opportunities across
  every asset class — and build durable, multi‑generational wealth.*

Recommended choice: **A1‑refined.** Once confirmed, this replaces the mission in
`docs/MISSION_STATEMENT.md` and the on‑site mission section.
