---

## Founder directives (definitive)

1. **JHI is a private Family Office.**
2. **JHI will NEVER manage outside funds or other people's money.** No custody, no
   discretionary management, no pooled vehicles, no assets under management for third parties.
3. **NO outside investors — ever.** JHI is founder-owned and self-funded; it will not raise
   outside equity or take venture/angel/PE capital.

These are non-negotiable constraints. All strategy, product, and documentation must conform.

---

## What JHI IS

- A **private family office** that builds and owns a **proprietary software platform** and
  research methodology for its own use.
- A **SaaS provider** that sells **research, education, and decision-support tools**
  (subscriptions) to B2C/B2B clients. Clients use the tools to make **their own** decisions.

## What JHI is NOT

- **Not** an investment manager, adviser-of-funds, broker-dealer, custodian, or fund.
- **Not** a manager of client or third-party money (never takes custody or discretion).
- **Not** a company seeking outside investors or fundraising.

---

## Why this simplifies the regulatory picture

Because JHI **never manages money and never custodies client assets**, the entire class of
**investment-manager / custody / fund** obligations does **not** apply.

**The one line that still matters (keep it clean):** selling software does not make you an
adviser, but giving **personalized investment advice for compensation** can implicate
investment-adviser rules **even without managing money**. So the platform must stay on the
**tools / research / education / decision-support** side — not personalized "buy this
security" advice. This is the same guardrail already documented:

- Market outputs (the Opportunity Score, rankings) as **analysis/education**, not advice.
- The score is **one input**, never a directive; include disclaimers.
- No promises of returns or performance.
- Confirm the advice-vs-tools line with counsel before client launch.

> Net: the **"managing money raises the bar / RIA registration / long live track record"**
> path is **off the table by design.** The remaining go-live requirement is (a) honest
> research validation (see `docs/RND_VALIDATION_PROTOCOL.md`) and (b) correct
> tools-not-advice positioning.
