## 1. Purpose
The Parties wish to explore and/or pursue **[the Purpose — e.g., a potential business
relationship, engagement, investment, or evaluation of the John Henry Investments
platform]** (the "**Purpose**"), and in connection with the Purpose may disclose
certain confidential and proprietary information to one another.

## 2. Confidential Information
"**Confidential Information**" means any non‑public information disclosed by the
Disclosing Party to the Receiving Party, in any form (written, oral, electronic,
visual), that is designated as confidential or that a reasonable person would
understand to be confidential given its nature or the circumstances of disclosure.
It includes, without limitation: software, source code, architecture, and APIs; the
John Henry Opportunity Score methodology, algorithms, models, and research; product
roadmaps; financial information, pricing, and projections; business, marketing, and
customer information; personal/customer data; trade secrets; and the terms of any
discussions between the Parties.

## 3. Exclusions
Confidential Information does **not** include information that the Receiving Party can
demonstrate: (a) was publicly available at the time of disclosure or later became
public through no breach of this Agreement; (b) was rightfully in its possession
without obligation of confidentiality before disclosure; (c) was rightfully received
from a third party without restriction; or (d) was independently developed without use
of or reference to the Disclosing Party's Confidential Information.

## 4. Obligations of the Receiving Party
The Receiving Party shall: (a) use the Confidential Information solely for the Purpose;
(b) protect it using at least the same degree of care it uses for its own confidential
information, and no less than reasonable care; (c) not disclose it to any third party
except to its employees, contractors, advisors, or representatives who have a
need‑to‑know for the Purpose and are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as
protective as these (the Receiving Party remains responsible for their compliance);
and (d) not reverse engineer, decompile, or disassemble any disclosed software or
materials except as permitted by law or written agreement.

## 5. Compelled Disclosure
If the Receiving Party is required by law, regulation, or valid legal process to
disclose Confidential Information, it may do so provided that (to the extent legally
permitted) it gives the Disclosing Party prompt written notice and reasonable
cooperation to seek protective treatment, and discloses only what is legally required.

## 6. Term and Survival
This Agreement begins on the Effective Date and continues for **[Term — e.g., two (2)
years]**, unless terminated earlier upon **[30]** days' written notice. The
confidentiality obligations in this Agreement survive for **[Survival Period — e.g.,
three (3) years]** after disclosure of the applicable Confidential Information;
provided that obligations with respect to trade secrets continue for as long as the
information remains a trade secret under applicable law.

## 7. Return or Destruction
Upon the Disclosing Party's written request or termination of this Agreement, the
Receiving Party shall promptly return or destroy all Confidential Information (and
copies), and certify such destruction in writing if requested, except for one archival
copy retained for legal/compliance purposes or copies in routine backups, which remain
subject to this Agreement.

## 8. No License; No Obligation
No license or other right to any intellectual property is granted under this Agreement,
whether by implication or otherwise. Nothing herein obligates either Party to proceed
with any transaction or relationship.

## 9. No Warranty
All Confidential Information is provided "**AS IS**." The Disclosing Party makes no
warranties, express or implied, regarding its accuracy or completeness.

## 10. Remedies
The Parties agree that unauthorized disclosure or use of Confidential Information may
cause irreparable harm for which monetary damages may be inadequate, and that the
Disclosing Party is entitled to seek injunctive or equitable relief in addition to any
other remedies available at law or in equity.

this matches your entity's state of formation/operations; change if needed)*, without

## 12. Miscellaneous
(a) **Entire Agreement.** This Agreement is the entire agreement between the Parties
regarding its subject matter and supersedes prior discussions. (b) **Amendment.** Any
amendment must be in writing and signed by both Parties. (c) **Assignment.** Neither
Party may assign this Agreement without the other's prior written consent, except to a
successor in connection with a merger or sale of substantially all assets. (d)
**Severability.** If any provision is held unenforceable, the remainder remains in
effect. (e) **No Waiver.** Failure to enforce any provision is not a waiver. (f)
**Counterparts / E‑Signature.** This Agreement may be executed in counterparts and by
electronic signature, each of which is deemed an original.
