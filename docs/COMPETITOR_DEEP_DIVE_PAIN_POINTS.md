## The single biggest oversight (our wedge, in their users' own words)
From an ETA/search-fund practitioner on diligence tooling:
> "A first-pass review of a data room, reading the CIM, tying it to the financials, normalizing how the seller presented their numbers, building a question list, is days of senior time per target. Most of it is spent on deals that die. For a two-person search fund … **that first pass is the bottleneck. Not judgment. Reading.**"
> "The tool should **make it harder to miss the line that matters** … show me the evidence faster … **kill the weak deals early.**"

**That is Deal X-Ray + QoE, exactly.** The incumbents sell *data*; nobody automates the **first-pass CIM read** that actually bottlenecks the searcher. And the economics scream for it:
- Search costs **$250k–$350k** over ~20 months; **~⅓ of searchers never acquire.**
- **33% of LOIs fail**; broken-deal reserve **~$50k**; **46.6% of dead deals die from diligence findings** — "the most preventable category."
- QoE is **$20k–$30k** and only run *after* LOI. There's a gaping hole for **cheap, early screening to "get to *no* fast"** before big spend — which is our Deal X-Ray pre-screen feeding an affordable, CPA-signed QoE.

## Per-competitor quick read
- **PitchBook:** best deal data, but "expensive," limited Excel plugin, weak public/credit data, stale records, painful renewals. *Oversight: no diligence layer; hostile to small buyers.*
- **CB Insights:** great predictive market intel, but **$40–60k+/yr**, tech-startup-centric, can't ingest your own docs, unintuitive search. *Oversight: not for SMB acquisition diligence at all.*
- **Capital IQ / FactSet:** deep + costly, enterprise-only, steep learning curve. *Oversight: overkill for searchers.*
- **Grata/SourceScrub:** strong AI sourcing, but **stop at sourcing**; custom pricing. *Oversight: no diligence/close.*
- **Mergr:** clean, focused, honest "Who uses" framing — a good *presentation* model to emulate; but it's a PE/M&A directory, not a diligence tool.
