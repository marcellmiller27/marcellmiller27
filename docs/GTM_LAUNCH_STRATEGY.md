# Go-To-Market & Launch Strategy — JHI Research & Analytics Firm, Inc.

**Owner:** Cy Henry (VP Software Engineering — AI) · **Signature:** `69M2705M`
**Context:** Once NASDAQ commences and **line-item 5H validates**, we launch and begin subscriptions.
This is the strategic roll-out: sales motion, marketing campaigns, and **partnerships with true
base-performance companies**. Living document; pairs with `docs/CONTRIBUTION_MARGIN_CVP.md`.

## 1. Positioning (one line)
**Institutional research and deal diligence — without the institutional price.** Multi-asset research
(Economics), a transparent Opportunity Score, CIM/Deal X-Ray (Scope), CPA-signable Quality of
Earnings, and a deal pipeline — for independent investors, acquirers, and the professionals who advise them.

## 2. Segments → tier → motion
| Segment | Tier | Motion |
|---|---|---|
| Retail investors / wealth builders | Consumer ($110/mo) | **Product-led** — free newsletter → self-serve checkout |
| Search-fund / SMB acquirers, advisors | Professional ($299/mo) | Product-led + light sales; content + community |
| Family offices, firms, CPAs, attorneys, bankers | Enterprise ($1,500/mo, 5 seats) | **Sales-led** — outreach, demo, seat expansion |

## 3. Funnel (free → paid), tied to what we've built
- **Top:** free **Newsletter** (Economic Brief · Red Alerts · Opportunity Scan) → email capture (leads store). Editorial is our owned-media engine.
- **Middle:** free **Opportunity Score** + teaser editions → register → trial of Scope/Earnings.
- **Bottom:** purchase flow (Phase A→B) → subscription; Enterprise via sales + seat expansion.
- **Instrument every step** (see CVP + funnel metrics) so CAC and conversion are known before we scale spend.

## 4. Marketing campaigns
1. **Owned media (lowest CAC, highest trust):** the newsletter as the flagship — weekly cadence, methodology-forward, Ivy-league voice (see `EDITORIAL_PROGRAM_ASSESSMENT.md`). Gate the full edition to drive signups.
2. **Founder/thought-leadership:** methodology explainers (Opportunity Score, QoE, valuation) — build authority vs. incumbents (42 Macro / Mergr comparables).
3. **Category SEO + comparison content:** "institutional research without the $30k contract / fake trial / renewal trap."
4. **Referral / affiliate:** reward advisors and power users (fits per-seat economics).
5. **Webinars / live teardowns:** a live Deal X-Ray / QoE walkthrough converts acquirers.

## 5. Partnerships with "true base-performance companies"
Partner with credible, high-performing firms whose users overlap ours — data, distribution, and advisory:
- **Data & infrastructure (credibility + capability):** NASDAQ/Sharadar (in progress), FRED/BEA/BLS (public), and a vetted market-data vendor. Co-marketing with recognized data brands lends institutional legitimacy.
- **Deal-flow / marketplaces (SMB acquirers):** business-for-sale marketplaces and broker networks (e.g., BizBuySell-class) — integrations + co-marketing put Scope/QoE in front of active buyers.
- **Advisory networks (Professional/Enterprise):** RIA, CPA, and M&A-advisor associations — bundle/reseller and the **partner-CPA network** for QoE sign-off (already envisioned in the Financial Diligence Suite).
- **Distribution / media:** finance newsletters, podcasts, and creators for sponsored placements to the retail tier.
- **Fintech rails:** Stripe (billing), and identity/e-sign for Enterprise onboarding.

**Partner selection bar:** proven performance + audience overlap + brand credibility + clean data governance (NASDAQ **no-spillage**). Structure as co-marketing, integration, referral/affiliate, or reseller — pick per partner.

## 6. Sales force (what's required)
- **Comp = variable selling cost per unit** → feeds the Contribution Margin model (`CONTRIBUTION_MARGIN_CVP.md`): the prepaid-MSA commission (15% upfront + year-end bonus) is a **variable cost per unit** and must be inside CM/break-even.
- **Ramp:** start focused on Enterprise/Family Office (highest CM per deal); scale reps against EBITDA-gated staffing (per the consolidated projections).
- **Enablement:** demo script (Scope → Earnings → Pipeline), ROI vs. the "$30k contract," seat-expansion playbook for Enterprise.

## 7. Launch sequence (gated on 5H)
1. Clear **NASDAQ 5H** + countersign Order Form.
2. Ship purchase flow (Phase A→B) + per-plan gating; storefront **copy rewrite**.
3. **Private beta** with design partners (advisors, a few family offices) → testimonials + case studies.
4. Turn on **newsletter email (Step B)** for owned-media distribution.
5. **Public launch:** campaigns live, partnerships announced, referral on.
6. Measure CAC/LTV/conversion against the CVP break-even; scale spend only where unit economics hold.

## 8. KPIs
Free signups → paid conversion %, CAC by channel, LTV/CAC, MRR by tier, seat expansion (Enterprise), newsletter engagement, break-even progress (units vs. target from CVP).
